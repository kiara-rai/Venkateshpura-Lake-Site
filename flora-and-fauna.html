Why biodiversity at the lake matters

Venkateshpura Lake functions as a living ecosystem: aquatic plants filter water, shoreline vegetation prevents erosion, and diverse fauna—from fish and frogs to dragonflies and water birds—keep food webs in balance. Healthy biodiversity improves water quality, supports pollinators, and offers opportunities for learning and recreation.

This page highlights notable flora and fauna observed around urban lakes like Venkateshpura, encouraging careful observation, documentation, and stewardship by the community.

  • Birds

    Often observed: Indian Spot-billed Duck, Little Cormorant, Cattle Egret, Pond Heron, White-throated Kingfisher. Seasonal visitors vary with water levels and food availability.

  • Fish

    Common groups include carps and small native species that feed birds and stabilize food webs. Vegetation cover supports breeding and offers refuge from predators.

  • Amphibians

    Frogs and toads breed during monsoon, controlling insects naturally and serving as bioindicators of water quality.

  • Insects

    Dragonflies, damselflies, butterflies, and pollinators thrive near flowering plants; their presence indicates balanced predator–prey relationships.

Notable regional sightings (occasionally recorded in urban Bengaluru lakes): Painted Stork and Spot-billed Pelican (both Near Threatened globally). If observed, maintain distance and avoid disturbance.

Seasonal changes across the year

Species presence and behavior shift with rainfall and temperature. Here’s what to look for:

Monsoon

  • Amphibian breeding and chorus calls during evenings.
  • Rapid growth of submerged and emergent plants.
  • Increased dragonfly activity and prey abundance.

Post-monsoon & Winter

  • Best time for birdwatching; migrants and higher diversity.
  • Flowering of aquatic and riparian plants attracts pollinators.
  • Stable water levels improve visibility of shoreline species.

Summer

  • Lower water exposes mudflats used by waders and herons.
  • Shade from lotus and lilies helps fish avoid heat stress.
  • Monitor algal growth; balanced vegetation aids water clarity.

How you can help conserve biodiversity

Community actions protect habitats and keep the lake thriving for future generations.

Reduce pollution at source

Avoid dumping waste or chemicals; join clean-ups and support sewage treatment and silt-traps in the catchment.

Restore native vegetation

Plant indigenous species along the bund and avoid introducing invasive plants that displace native flora.

Observe responsibly

Keep a respectful distance from nesting birds and amphibian breeding sites; avoid loud noises and feeding wildlife.

Document and share

Record sightings on community boards or citizen-science platforms to support monitoring and education.
