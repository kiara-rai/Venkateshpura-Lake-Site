Lakes in Cities

With the rise of urbanization, the infrastructure of cities is changing from green ecosystems to concrete jungles. The construction of new urban infrastructure slowly leads to the encroachment of water bodies in the cities.

Water bodies play various roles in major urban cities, especially Bangalore. However, they have deteriorated over the last few decades, with disappearance of majority of the lakes, drastic shrinkage of the rest, and pollution and water contamination in all.

Roles Played By Lakes

Recognizing the importance of the lakes in Bengaluru is essential to understanding the impact of their deterioration. They are a key presence in the city, holding important historical, cultural, social, hydrological, economical, and environmental significance.

  1. Historical

    The lakes of Bengaluru carry a rich cultural heritage. They were built by various rulers over the centuries, such as the Cholas, Hoysalas, and Kempegowda. These water bodies were later expanded and adapted under the Mysore kings and further shaped during British colonial rule.

  2. Ecological

    Lakes contribute to the city’s microclimate by cooling temperatures and improving air quality. They support a wide range of flora and fauna including diverse aquatic life and bird species, creating a vital balance in the ecosystem. By acting as natural reservoirs by absorbing rainwater, lakes play a major role in preventing floods during the monsoons. Recharging groundwater, these water bodies sustain open wells and borewells in the surrounding area.

  3. Water Supply

    Since Bengaluru does not have a perennial river, lakes are important as a water source. The city gets its water supply from Cauvery river, situated around 108 km away. Thus, lakes hold a significant portion of the water sources. (Birawat et al., 2021). They reduce the city’s dependence on the Cauvery river, providing an easier alternative by supplying groundwater.

  4. Beautification

    Aside from these practical roles played by the water bodies, they also beautify the city. Their value and beauty is a staple of Bengaluru, attracting tourists, keeping property rates slightly higher thus contributing to the economy, as well as improving the quality of life for the citizens. Overall, they are essential to the identity of the city and its charm

  5. Humanitarian

    Lakes are of great importance to the people of the city as they support livelihoods of fishermen, dhobis (washermen or washerwomen), grazers, fodder collectors, and more. Eutrophication and water pollution can be fatal to aquatic life, which leads to many fishermen losing their livelihoods.

Causes of deterioration

The gradual degradation of water bodies can be linked not only to environmental phenomena, but also to harmful activities performed by various stakeholders in cities.
